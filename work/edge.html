Edge: AI creative sparring tool

A four-phase creative sparring session powered by Claude AI: Dump, Challenge, Provocation and Commit. Each with a distinct prompt personality designed to push designers past their defaults. Every phase has its own visual language that makes the conversation feel like the work itself. The session ends with an AI-generated artifact: a complete creative brief including colour palette, typography direction, principles, and visual references.

The Problem

Speed pressure has made designers safe. With AI tools making "good enough" faster than ever, most designers skip the hardest part — the thinking before the making. Edge exists to restore that phase: the uncomfortable, generative territory between having an idea and defaulting to the familiar.

AI Architecture

Four engineered system prompts: The Listener, The Challenger, The Provocateur, The Synthesiser. Each with a distinct voice, constraint set, and creative job.

Key Product Decisions
One intelligence, four modes
Rather than a general AI assistant, Edge has four distinct prompt personalities, each engineered to do a specific job at a specific moment in the creative process.
Structure over open-endedness
A time-boxed four-step arc prevents the session from becoming an endless conversation. Designers always know where they are and where they're going.
The artifact as the output
Every other AI tool ends with text. Edge ends with a visual creative brief — something a designer can pin to their wall or share with their team.
Atmosphere as UX
Inspiration isn't saved for the end. The grain texture, ambient movement, tone switcher, and phase-specific formatting make every step feel like the work itself.
