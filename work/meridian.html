The Bet

The company was under pressure to invest in a Digital Sales Room — driven by analyst hype, internal revenue narratives, and a marquee enterprise customer.

"Turn an abstract, hype-driven idea into something concrete enough to validate — or stop."

What the
evidence showed

We were mistaking conceptual enthusiasm for readiness. No evidence of adoption intent. No timing conviction. No internal capability to support rollout. False validation would have converted enthusiasm into sunk cost.

"Interest doesn't equal readiness to change entrenched workflows."

What changed

Leadership grounded strategy directly in customer operating reality. Investment shifted from speculative abstractions to foundational capabilities. Roadmap redirected toward self-service and CPQ improvements — the infrastructure adoption actually requires.
